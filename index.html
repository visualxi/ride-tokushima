Routes shaped by the island.

Tokushima gives riders a rare mix: open river flats, sea-air coastal roads, fishing-town lanes, technical mountain climbs, and deep forest valleys found nowhere else in Western Shikoku.

Flagship Route

Naruto Coastal Circuit

JR Naruto Station out along Uchinoumi's fishing lanes, over rolling headlands, and back through Naruto Park above the whirlpools. The primary guided inbound route — social pace, photo stops, and a coastal café finish.

52–85km 430–750m Sea views Guided available
Urban Climb

Mt. Bizan

Short, sharp, and close to the city. A compact test piece before coffee near Tokushima Station — 620m in 28km with a timed summit effort.

28km 620m
Remote Valley

Iya Valley

Mountain roads, vine bridges, and gorge scenery for riders who want a full-day escape. Almost no English documentation exists — genuinely undiscovered territory.

Coming 2027

Onaruto Bridge.
Cycling path opens.

The Onaruto Bridge — spanning the whirlpool straits between Awaji Island and Tokushima — opens to cyclists in 2027. Japan's first cycling crossing over this iconic structure. Ride Tokushima is building towards this as its primary brand milestone.

The prefecture — 徳島県

Shikoku's cycling blind spot.

Tokushima sits on the northeast corner of Shikoku — between the Yoshino River to the north, the Anan coastline to the south, and the Tsurugi massif rising to nearly 2,000 metres at its centre. Most visiting cyclists pass through on pilgrimage. Almost none stop to ride.

That's changing. The prefecture has a national cycle route designation along the Yoshino River, the longest forest road in Japan running through the Tsurugi mountains, a zero-waste village used as a cycling basecamp, and a coastal riding scene that rivals anything in Western Japan — all with a fraction of the traffic and tourist infrastructure of Kyoto or Hiroshima.
